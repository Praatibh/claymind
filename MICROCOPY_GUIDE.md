## ðŸ“ Writing Guidelines

### Sentence Structure

**Do:**
- Keep sentences under 15 words when possible
- Use active voice ("You earned this" not "This was earned by you")
- Start with the most important information
- Use "you" and "your" to make it personal
- Vary sentence beginnings (don't always start with "You...")

**Don't:**
- Use passive constructions
- Bury the main point in the middle
- Use complex subordinate clauses
- String together long lists in one sentence

**Examples:**
- âŒ "Upon successful completion of the mission, achievements will be unlocked for you."
- âœ… "You completed the mission! Here's what you unlocked."

---

### Word Choice

**Use these words:**
- Learn, explore, discover, build, create, try
- Curious, careful, thoughtful, creative
- Real, actual, everyday, true
- Help, guide, show, explain
- Safe, protected, private

**Avoid these words:**
- Leverage, utilize, optimize, maximize
- Synergy, ecosystem, platform, solution
- Master, dominate, crush, destroy
- Always, never, must, have to (unless safety)
- Revolutionary, amazing, incredible (overused superlatives)

---

### Numbers & Data

**Do:**
- Round to friendly numbers ("about 15 minutes" not "14.7 minutes")
- Use digits for numbers 10+ (10, 25, 100)
- Spell out numbers under 10 (three, seven, nine)
- Show progress as fractions when helpful (7 out of 10)
- Use percentages sparingly

**Examples:**
- âœ… "About 15 minutes"
- âœ… "7 out of 8 correct"
- âœ… "You're Level 5"
- âŒ "87.5% accuracy rate"
- âŒ "Approximately 14 minutes, 32 seconds"

---

### Punctuation & Formatting

**Do:**
- Use contractions (you're, we'll, don't) for warmth
- End most messages with periods, not exclamation marks
- Use exclamation marks only for real achievements
- Use question marks for genuine questions
- Use em dashes (â€”) for emphasis or clarification

**Don't:**
- Overuse exclamation marks!!!!!
- Use ellipses to create suspense...
- Use ALL CAPS except for acronyms
- Use semicolons (too formal for kids)

**Examples:**
- âœ… "You did it! That was tricky."
- âœ… "Hmm, that didn't work. Want to try again?"
- âŒ "Great job!!! You're amazing!!!"
- âŒ "Loading... please wait..."

## ðŸ§’ Age-Appropriate Language (7-14 years)

### Vocabulary Guidelines

**7-9 years:**
- Very simple, concrete words
- Short sentences (under 12 words)
- Everyday examples (school, games, friends)
- Minimal technical terms (explain when used)

**10-12 years:**
- Can handle more abstract concepts
- Sentences up to 15 words
- Can understand "bias," "algorithm," "data"
- Still need examples and visuals

**13-14 years:**
- More sophisticated vocabulary okay
- Can handle nuance and complexity
- Respect their growing independence
- Don't condescend

**Universal (all ages):**
- Define technical terms on first use
- Use analogies to familiar concepts
- Break complex ideas into steps
- Assume intelligence, not prior knowledge

---

### Examples by Age Group

**Explaining "Algorithm" (age-appropriate variations):**

**7-9 years:**
> "An algorithm is like a recipe. It's step-by-step instructions that tell a computer what to do."

**10-12 years:**
> "An algorithm is a set of rules that tells AI how to solve a problem, like how to recommend videos you might like."

**13-14 years:**
> "An algorithm is a process that AI uses to make decisions. For example, the algorithm in your video app looks at what you've watched before and suggests similar content."
